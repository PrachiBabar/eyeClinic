Understanding Dry Eye

Dry Eye occurs when your eyes don't produce enough tears, or when tears evaporate too quickly. Left unmanaged, it can affect comfort, vision, and daily life — but it responds very well to the right treatment plan.

What is dry eye?

When your tear film can't keep up

Dry Eye is a common condition that occurs when your eyes do not produce enough tears or when the tears evaporate too quickly. Tears are essential for keeping the eyes lubricated, comfortable, and protected.

Without a healthy tear film, the eyes can become irritated, inflamed, and prone to infection. Dry Eye can affect people of all ages and may become more common with age, prolonged screen use, certain medications, or underlying medical conditions.

What you may notice

Symptoms of Dry Eye

Sensation & discomfort

  • Dryness or a gritty sensation in the eyes
  • Burning or stinging eyes
  • Itchy eyes
  • A feeling of something stuck in the eye

Visual & visible signs

  • Redness and irritation
  • Blurred or fluctuating vision
  • Sensitivity to light
  • Excessive watering (a reflex response to dryness)
  • Eye fatigue, especially after screens or reading
  • Difficulty wearing contact lenses

Treatment for Dry Eye

Treatment depends on the underlying cause and severity of the condition.

Artificial Tears

Lubricating eye drops help keep the eyes moist and relieve dryness and irritation.

Prescription Medications

Anti-inflammatory drops or other medication to improve tear production and reduce inflammation.

Warm Compresses & Eyelid Hygiene

Improves oil gland function and reduces tear evaporation.

Punctal Plugs

Tiny plugs inserted into the tear ducts to reduce drainage and keep eyes lubricated longer.

Lifestyle Modifications

Simple daily habits that ease symptoms:

  • ◎ 20-20-20 rule for screen breaks
  • ◎ Stay well hydrated
  • ◎ Avoid direct fan / AC exposure
  • ◎ Use a humidifier in dry rooms
  • ◎ Wear protective eyewear outdoors

Meibosoft Therapy

An advanced, non-invasive treatment for Meibomian Gland Dysfunction (MGD), a leading cause of Dry Eye. It uses controlled therapeutic heat to soften and clear blocked oil glands, restoring the tear film's natural oil layer.

  • ✦ Non-invasive and comfortable
  • ✦ Improves meibomian gland function
  • ✦ Quick treatment with no downtime

Why treat dry eye early

Left unmanaged, chronic dryness can damage the ocular surface and increase the risk of corneal complications. A tailored plan restores comfort and protects long-term eye health.
