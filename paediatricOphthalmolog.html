What is paediatric ophthalmology?

Eye care built around a growing child

Paediatric Ophthalmology is the branch of eye care that focuses on the diagnosis, treatment, and management of eye conditions in infants, children, and adolescents.

Since a child's vision develops rapidly during the early years, regular eye examinations are essential to detect and treat vision problems before they affect learning, development, and overall quality of life.

Good to know: Many childhood eye conditions can be successfully treated when diagnosed early.

What we treat

Common Childhood Eye Conditions

Our paediatric eye specialists diagnose and treat a wide range of eye conditions.

◎

Refractive Errors

Myopia, Hyperopia, and Astigmatism affecting focus.

◎

Squint (Strabismus)

Misaligned eyes that don't point in the same direction.

◎

Lazy Eye (Amblyopia)

Reduced vision in one eye due to abnormal visual development.

◎

Congenital Cataract & Glaucoma

Lens clouding or raised eye pressure present from birth.

◎

Blocked Tear Duct

Nasolacrimal duct obstruction causing watery, sticky eyes.

◎

Eye Allergies & Infections

Common childhood irritations needing prompt medical care.

◎

Drooping Eyelids (Ptosis)

A lowered upper eyelid that may affect vision if severe.

◎

ROP Screening

Retinopathy of Prematurity screening for premature babies.

Signs parents should watch for

Symptoms to Watch For

Seek an eye examination if your child shows any of the following.

Behavioural signs

  • Frequent eye rubbing
  • Squinting or closing one eye
  • Holding books or screens very close
  • Frequent headaches due to eye strain
  • Poor performance at school related to vision

Visible signs

  • Misaligned eyes (crossed or wandering eyes)
  • Difficulty seeing distant or near objects
  • Excessive tearing or watery eyes
  • Red or swollen eyes
  • Sensitivity to light
  • White reflex in the pupil

Care by age and condition

Paediatric Eye Care & Treatment

Comprehensive Eye Exam

Detailed evaluation of vision, alignment, focusing ability, and overall eye health.

Prescription Glasses

Corrective lenses for myopia, hyperopia, and astigmatism.

Amblyopia Treatment

Glasses, eye patching, atropine drops, or vision therapy to strengthen the weaker eye.

Squint Management

Glasses, prism lenses, eye exercises, botulinum toxin injections, or surgery where appropriate.

Medical Management

Eye drops or medications for infections, allergies, inflammation, or glaucoma.

Surgical Treatment

For congenital cataract, congenital glaucoma, squint, blocked tear ducts, or drooping eyelids when necessary.

Regular follow-up ensures healthy visual development and timely treatment adjustments as your child grows.
